Fox

Fox is a common name for many species of carnivorous mammals belonging to the Canidae family. Foxes are small to medium-sized canids (slightly smaller than the median-sized domestic dog), characterized by possessing a long narrow snout, and a bushy tail (or brush).

Members of about 37 species are referred to as foxes, of which only 12 species belong to the monophyletic "true foxes" group of the genus Vulpes. The most common and widespread species of fox is the red fox (Vulpes vulpes), although various types of fox can be found on almost every continent. The presence of the red fox in Australia, however, is considered introduced by humans.
